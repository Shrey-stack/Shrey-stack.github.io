When it comes to computer technology, new and modern is always going to be faster than old and outdated. That’s just a fact of life in today’s fast changing world of technology. Each and every version of Microsoft Windows naturally slows down over time as the Registry gets clogged up and corrupted and more programs and data files are squeezed onto the hard drive. I know that's sad.... But you know what's more sad??? Getting rid of it or complaining 24-7 when you can easily change its operating system from Windows to Linux.

So what did I do???


I created a bootable USB stick from my Windows and installed Ubuntu. Few things to consider are as follows:
* Atleast a 4 GB USB stick
* An Ubuntu flavor ISO file. I downloaded mine from Download Ubuntu
* A portable application. I downloaded rufus from Download Rufus
* Once your portable application is downloaded, you select your USB key, and in boot selection, you select your downloaded ISO image, MBR is partition scheme and BIOS in target system.
* Click start, and select write in ISO image mode
* Click OK and you're good to go

Start installation of ubuntu in your old laptop

* Turn on your old laptop.
* Now attach the flash drive to a USB port and press the "F11" key (for a Supermicro motherboard) during the boot process. As soon as the boot menu appears, select your stick and the installation will start.
* Click on the third option.
* You need to select basic things such as language, keyboard, your location etc. Once it is done, the installation begins.
* As soon as the installation has been completed successfully, Restart your laptop and
Voilà, you're done!!

Being a business student, I never ever thought I'd be doing something like this.... But this feels so good!!! Please give it a try (((:
